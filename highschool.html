College Preparatory Excellence

Our High School program serves students in Grades 9-12 with a comprehensive college preparatory curriculum that challenges students academically while developing critical thinking, leadership, and character.

We offer a wide range of Advanced Placement courses, honors classes, and electives that allow students to pursue their academic interests in depth. Our dedicated college counseling program begins in freshman year and provides personalized guidance throughout the college application process.

Beyond academics, we emphasize leadership development, community engagement, and the cultivation of lifelong learning habits that prepare students for success in university and beyond.

Our High School Curriculum

Advanced Placement

We offer 15+ AP courses across disciplines including Calculus, Biology, Chemistry, Physics, English Literature, U.S. History, and Computer Science.

Honors Program

Honors courses provide accelerated learning opportunities in core subjects for students seeking additional academic challenge.

Arts & Electives

Comprehensive arts program including visual arts, music, theater, and digital media, plus specialized electives in various disciplines.

College & Career Prep

Dedicated courses in college essay writing, financial literacy, public speaking, and career exploration to prepare students for life after high school.

Comprehensive College Counseling

Our four-year college counseling program begins in 9th grade and provides personalized guidance to help students find the right college fit and navigate the application process successfully.

Key components of our program include:

  • Individual meetings with college counselors starting in freshman year
  • College planning workshops for students and families
  • PSAT, SAT, and ACT preparation and testing opportunities
  • Assistance with college essays, applications, and interviews
  • College representative visits to campus
  • Financial aid and scholarship guidance

Our graduates have been accepted to top universities worldwide, including Ivy League institutions, liberal arts colleges, and specialized programs.

Leadership Development

We believe leadership skills are essential for success in college and beyond. Our comprehensive leadership program includes:

Student Government

Elected student leaders plan events, manage budgets, and represent student interests while developing practical leadership skills.

Peer Mentoring

Upperclassmen mentor younger students, providing academic support and guidance while developing communication and empathy skills.

Community Service

Students complete 100+ hours of community service, developing civic responsibility and leadership through meaningful engagement.

Leadership Retreats

Annual leadership retreats focus on team-building, communication, and developing personal leadership styles.

Graduation Requirements

To graduate from Prima Academy High School, students must complete the following requirements:

English: 4 years (including Literature and Composition)
Mathematics: 4 years (through at least Pre-Calculus)
Science: 4 years (including Biology, Chemistry, and Physics)
Social Studies: 4 years (including World History and U.S. History)
World Language: 3 years of the same language
Arts: 2 years of visual or performing arts
Physical Education: 2 years
Electives: 6 additional credits
Community Service: 100+ hours
Senior Project: Capstone research and presentation
